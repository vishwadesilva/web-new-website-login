I THREATS FACED BY THIS SPECIES

1. Habitat Loss and Fragmentation

Consequences: Lankan leopards face significant habitat loss due to human activities such as agriculture, urbanization, and infrastructure development. This results in the fragmentation of their natural habitats, isolating populations and reducing the availability of prey.

Mechanisms to Reduce the Threat: Conservation organizations and government agencies in Sri Lanka are working to establish and maintain protected areas, corridors, and buffer zones to connect fragmented habitats. This helps maintain genetic diversity and ensures the leopards' ability to roam freely, reducing the impact of habitat loss.



2. Human-Wildlife Conflict

Consequences: As human populations expand into leopard habitats, conflicts arise over competition for resources. Leopards may prey on livestock, leading to retaliatory killings by local communities. This exacerbates the strain on leopard populations and fuels negative perceptions of the species.

Mechanisms to Reduce the Threat: Conservation initiatives include community-based programs that raise awareness about coexisting with leopards. Implementing secure livestock enclosures, providing compensation for livestock losses, and employing early-warning systems can help mitigate conflicts, fostering a more harmonious relationship between leopards and local communities.



3. Poaching and Illegal Wildlife Trade

Consequences: Sri Lankan leopard faces the threat of poaching for its skin, bones, and other body parts, driven by illegal wildlife trade. This activity poses a direct threat to the survival of the species and contributes to the decline of leopard populations.

Mechanisms to Reduce the Threat: Sri Lanka has implemented stricter law enforcement measures against poaching and illegal wildlife trade. Enhanced patrolling in protected areas, intelligence gathering, and collaboration with international agencies are critical components of these efforts. Additionally, educational programs and awareness campaigns are conducted to inform the public about the consequences of wildlife trade and the importance of leopard conservation.
